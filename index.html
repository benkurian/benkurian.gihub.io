Cookies, made the clever way.

 Have you ever felt completely swindled when more of the ice cream from your ice cream sandwich lands on the floor instead of your mouth? Well, brings you a solution: Cookie Rolls! Cookie Rolls are the mouthwatering image of freezing cold ice cream wrapped in a delicious cookie blanket. Imagine an ice cream cookie sandwich with none of the mess, these delectable Cookie Rolls are now available to all cookie lovers worldwide!

 The first persuasive technique used in our ad is celebrity endorsement. The way we got this persuasive technique across is putting Cookie Monster himself (from Sesame Street) in our ad quoting his support for Cookie Rolls (“Forget other cookie, this cookie hit different”). The reason we put this specific persuasive technique in our ad is because we hope that once people see the most funny, popular character in Sesame Street they will associate our product with cookie monster, further increasing our product’s popularity. This form of celebrity endorsement is effective because everyone wants to be like their favorite childhood character.

 The second persuasive technique we used in our ad is bribery. We quoted “Buy one get one. (for the same price).” We utilized this form of persuasion in our print ad because we could take the advantage of the fact that most people don’t read the fine print. When seeing, “buy one, get one,” people automatically think that they can get another for the same price, therefore motivating them more to get our product in the short term. But, to our benefit the ad stated it was for the same price so we get profits rather than the downside of having to give someone our product for free.

 In our website, we used two extra persuasive techniques. The third persuasive technique we used is repetition. We formed our repetition into a very original song that’s bound to engrave Cookie Rolls into your brain. Hearing our product name and our brand over and over will make it hard to forget about delicious Cookie Rolls. This persuasive technique works only because humans remember something they hear multiple times because they are exposed to it for a longer period of time. As a bonus, repetition mixed into a catchy song will make sure the product is stuck in viewers’ heads for extra persuasion.

 The fourth and final persuasive technique we used in our product was sensory appeal. Some of the ways we did this was incorporating a bright color scheme into our advertisement and also making a 3D animation of our cookie roll spinning around so the target audience could see all the textures and colors of our product at any angle. When people are pleased with what they see in a product or ad, they will most likely want to buy that product because they will associate our product with our colorful advertisement and think our product is just as appealing as we made it up to be.
